## Research Summary

### What is OCR?
OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is a technology that helps computers read text from images, scanned papers, or photos. Instead of typing everything by hand, OCR can automatically convert the text into an editable digital format. It makes working with documents much faster and more convenient.

### Why is Urdu OCR harder than English OCR?
Urdu OCR is more challenging because Urdu is written in a connected script, and the shape of each letter can change depending on where it appears in a word. Many Urdu letters also look almost the same, with only small dots making the difference. Because of this, along with different fonts and handwriting styles, it is much harder for a computer to recognize Urdu text accurately.

### Real-world uses of Urdu OCR
Urdu OCR would be very useful for converting old Urdu books, newspapers, and historical records into digital text so they can be searched and preserved. It can also help businesses and government offices quickly extract text from Urdu forms, invoices, or official documents without entering everything manually. This saves time and reduces mistakes.
## Why We Need a Better Model?
Tesseract fails on Urdu because it struggles to recognize the complex structure of Urdu characters and words. It often produces incorrect or empty results, especially when the images contain different fonts, noise, or low-quality text. A dedicated Urdu OCR model is needed to achieve more accurate and reliable text recognition.
